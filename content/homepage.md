# History is for everyone.

The American Revolution didn't happen in a textbook. It happened in towns—in meeting houses and taverns, on village greens and farm roads, in the lives of ordinary people who became part of an extraordinary story.

**Sabrina's Town** connects you to the 75 most important Revolutionary towns, weaving their stories together into a living network of history. Not monuments to be admired from a distance, but places to be explored, questioned, and understood.

We believe history belongs to everyone—travelers seeking meaning in their journeys, teachers bringing the past alive for students, and communities preserving their heritage for future generations. We believe the well-known stories matter, but so do the voices that history too often forgot: the women, the enslaved, the Native peoples, the dissenters, the ordinary folk whose names didn't make it into the textbooks.

Every town in our network links to others—shared events, shared people, shared themes, shared routes. Because the Revolution wasn't isolated incidents; it was a web of connections stretching from Massachusetts to Georgia, from 1765 to 1783 and beyond. Understanding any single thread means following it to others.

And we believe history should be honest. When we're certain, we say so. When we're relying on tradition or anecdote, we say that too. When questions remain open, we ask them with you.

## How the Network Works

### Interconnected Towns

Every town links to 5–8 others with explicit reasons: shared events, shared historical figures, thematic connections, or geographic routes. Click from Lexington to Concord to Boston to Philadelphia—follow the threads of the Revolution wherever they lead.

### Living Updates

History isn't static, and neither are we. We continuously monitor reputable sources—from the National Park Service to university press releases to local historical societies—for new research, new discoveries, and new perspectives. Every update is logged transparently, with change notes explaining what changed and why.

### Verified Stories

Not all historical claims are equally certain. We label every story:
- **Verified**: Strong documentary evidence supports this account
- **Oral Tradition**: Based on accounts passed down, not contemporary documentation
- **Anecdotal**: Individual recollections that may be embellished
- **Unverified**: Included for completeness, but evidence is thin

This isn't about dismissing traditions—it's about being honest about what we know and how we know it.

### Scored and Ranked

Our composite scoring system evaluates each town across multiple dimensions: historical significance, preservation quality, accessibility, educational resources, interconnection with the network, diversity of stories, and quality of sourcing. Scores are transparent, and every town can see how to improve.
